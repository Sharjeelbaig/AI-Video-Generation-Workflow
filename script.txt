<Heading>Location and Nature of Arab Tribes.<Heading>
---
Beyond a shadow of doubt,
---
the biography of Prophet مُحَمَّد Peace and blessings be upon him manifestedly represents an exhaustive embodiment of the sublime Divine Message
---
that he communicated in order to deliver the human race from the swamp of darkness and polytheism
---
to the paradise of light and monotheism. An image, authentic as well as comprehensive, of this Message is therefore only attainable through careful study
---
and profound analysis of both backgrounds and issues of such a biography. 
---
In view of this,
---
a whole chapter is here introduced about the nature and development of Arab tribes prior to Islam as well as the circumstantial environment that enwrapped the Prophet's mission.
---
<SubHeading>LOCATION OF THE ARABS:</SubHeading>
---
Linguistically,
---
the word عَرَب means deserts and waste barren land well-nigh waterless and treeless. Ever since the dawn of history,
---
the Arabian Peninsula and its people have been called as such.
---
The Arabian Peninsula is enclosed in the west by the Red Sea and Sinai,
---
in the east by the Arabian Gulf,
---
in the south by the Arabian Sea,
---
which is an extension of the Indian Ocean,
---
and in the north by old شَام and part of عِرَاق. The area is estimated between a million and a million and a quarter square miles.
---
Thanks to its geographical position,
---
the peninsula has always maintained great importance. Considering its internal setting,
---
it is mostly deserts and sandy places,
---
which has rendered it inaccessible to foreigners and invaders,
---
and allowed its people complete liberty and independence through the ages,
---
despite the presence of two neighbouring great empires.
---
Its external setting,
---
on the other hand,
---
caused it to be the centre of the old world and provided it with sea and land links with most nations at the time. Thanks to this strategic position the Arabian Peninsula had become the centre for trade,
---
culture,
---
religion and art.
---
<SubHeading>ARAB TRIBES:</SubHeading>
---
Arab kinfolks have been divided according to lineage into three groups: 
---
Perishing Arabs,
---
The ancient Arabs of whose history little is known,
---
and of whom were عَاد,
---
ثَمُود,
---
طَسْم,
---
جَدِيس,
---
عَمَالِيق,
---
and others.
---
Pure Arabs,
---
Who originated from the progeny of يَعْرُب بْن يَشْجُب بْن قَحْطَان. They were also called قَحْطَانِيُّون. 
---
Arabized Arabs: Who originated from the progeny of إِسْمَاعِيل. They were also called عَدْنَانِيُّون.
---
The pure Arabs - the people of قَحْطَان - originally lived in يَمَن and comprised many tribes,
---
two of which were very famous:  حِمْيَر: The most famous of whose septs were زَيْد الجُمْهُور,
---
قُضَاعَة and سَكَاسِك.  كَهْلَان: The most famous of whose septs were هَمْدَان,
---
أَنْمَار,
---
طَيِّئ,
---
مَذْحِج,
---
كِنْدَة,
---
لَخْم,
---
جُذَام,
---
أَزْد,
---
أَوْس,
---
خَزْرَج and the descendants of جَفْنَة - the kings of old شَام.
---
كَهْلَان septs emigrated from يَمَن to dwell in the different parts of the Arabian Peninsula prior to the Great Flood (سَيْل العَرِم of مَأْرِب Dam),
---
due to the failure of trade under the Roman pressure and domain on both sea and land trade routes following Roman occupation of مِصْر and شَام. Naturally enough,
---
the competition between كَهْلَان and حِمْيَر led to the evacuation of the first and the settlement of the second in يَمَن.
---
THE EMIGRATING SEPTS OF كَهْلَان CAN BE INTO FOUR GROUPS:
---
 أَزْد: Who, under the leadership of عِمْرَان بْن عَمْرُو مُزَيْقِيَاء,
---
wandered in يَمَن,
---
sent pioneers and finally headed northwards. Details of their emigration can be summed up as follows:  ثَعْلَبَة بْن عَمْرُو left his tribe أَزْد for حِجَاز and dwelt between ثَعْلَبِيَّة and ذِي قَار. When he gained strength,
---
he headed for مَدِينَة where he stayed. Of his seed are أَوْس and خَزْرَج,
---
sons of حَارِثَة بْن ثَعْلَبَة.
---
حَارِثَة بْن عَمْرُو,
---
known as خُزَاعَة,
---
wandered with his folks in حِجَاز until they came to مَرّ الظَّهْرَان. Later,
---
they conquered the حَرَم,
---
and settled in مَكَّة after having driven away its people,
---
the tribe of جُرْهُم.
---
عِمْرَان بْن عَمْرُو and his folks went to عُمَان where they established the tribe of أَزْد whose children inhabited تِهَامَة and were known as أَزْد شَنُوءَة.
---
جَفْنَة بْن عَمْرُو and his family,
---
headed for شَام where he settled and initiated the kingdom of غَسَّان who was so named after a spring of water,
---
in حِجَاز,
---
where they stopped on their way to شَام.
---
 لَخْم and جُذَام: Of whom was نَصْر بْن رَبِيعَة,
---
father of مَنَاذِرَة,
---
Kings of حِيرَة.  بَنُو طَيِّئ: Who also emigrated northwards to settle by the so-called أَجَا and سَلْمَى Mountains which were consequently named as طَيِّئ Mountains.  كِنْدَة: Who dwelt in بَحْرَيْن but were expelled to حَضْرَمَوْت and نَجْد where they instituted a powerful government but not for long,
---
for the whole tribe soon faded away.
---
Another tribe of حِمْيَر,
---
known as قُضَاعَة,
---
also left يَمَن and dwelt in سَمَاوَة semi-desert on the borders of عِرَاق.
---
The Arabized Arabs go back in ancestry to their great grandfather إِبْرَاهِيم (Peace and blessings be upon him) from a town called آرِ near كُوفَة on the west bank of the Euphrates in عِرَاق. Excavations brought to light great details of the town,
---
إِبْرَاهِيم's family,
---
and the prevalent religions and social circumstances.
---
It is known that إِبْرَاهِيم (Peace and blessings be upon him) left آرِ for حَرَّان and then for فِلَسْطِين,
---
which he made headquarters for his Message. He wandered all over the area. When he went to مِصْر,
---
the فِرْعَوْن tried to do evil to his wife سَارَة,
---
but Allah saved her and the فِرْعَوْن's wicked scheme recoiled on him. He thus came to realize her strong attachment to Allah,
---
and,
---
in acknowledgment of her grace,
---
the فِرْعَوْن rendered his daughter هَاجَر at سَارَة's service,
---
but سَارَة gave هَاجَر to إِبْرَاهِيم as a wife. إِبْرَاهِيم returned to فِلَسْطِين where هَاجَر gave birth to إِسْمَاعِيل. سَارَة became so jealous of هَاجَر that she forced إِبْرَاهِيم to send هَاجَر and her baby away to a plantless valley on a small hill in حِجَاز,
---
by the Sacred House,
---
exposed to the wearing of floods coming right and left. He chose for them a place under a lofty tree above زَمْزَم near the upper side of the Mosque in مَكَّة where neither people nor water was available,
---
and went back to فِلَسْطِين leaving with his wife and baby a leather case with some dates and a pot of water. Not before long,
---
they ran out of both food and water,
---
but thanks to Allah's favour water gushed forth to sustain them for sometime. The whole story of زَمْزَم spring is already known to everybody.
---
Another Yemeni tribe - جُرْهُم the Second - came and lived in مَكَّة upon هَاجَر's permission,
---
after being said to have lived in the valleys around مَكَّة. It is mentioned in the صَحِيح البُخَارِي that this tribe came to مَكَّة before إِسْمَاعِيل was a young man while they had passed through that valley long before this event. إِبْرَاهِيم used to go to مَكَّة every now and then to see his wife and son. The number of these journeys is still unknown,
---
but authentic historical resources spoke of four ones.
---
Allah,
---
the Sublime,
---
stated in the Noble القُرْآن that He had إِبْرَاهِيم see,
---
in his dream,
---
that he slaughtered his son إِسْمَاعِيل,
---
and therefore إِبْرَاهِيم stood up to fulfill His Order:
---
'Then,
---
when they had both submitted themselves (to the Will of Allah),
---
and he had laid him prostrate on his forehead (or on the side of his forehead for slaughtering); and We called out to him: O إِبْرَاهِيم! You have fulfilled the dream (vision)! Verily! Thus do we reward the المُحْسِنُون (good-doers,
---
who perform good deeds totally for Allah's sake only,
---
without any show off or to gain praise or fame,
---
etc. and do them in accordance to Allah's Orders). Verily,
---
that indeed was a manifest trial - and We ransomed him with a great sacrifice (i.e. a ram)' [verse Reference is Qur'an's Chapter number 37, verse 103 to 107]
---
It is mentioned in the Genesis that إِسْمَاعِيل was thirteen years older than his brother إِسْحَاق. The sequence of the story of the sacrifice of إِسْمَاعِيل shows that it really happened before إِسْحَاق's birth,
---
and that Allah's Promise to give إِبْرَاهِيم another son,
---
إِسْحَاق,
---
came after narration of the whole story. This story spoke of one journey - at least - before إِسْمَاعِيل became a young man. البُخَارِي,
---
on the authority of اِبْن عَبَّاس,
---
reported the other three journeys; a summary of which goes as follows: When إِسْمَاعِيل became a young man,
---
he learned Arabic at the hand of the tribe of جُرْهُم,
---
who loved him with great admiration and gave him one of their women as a wife,
---
soon after his mother died. Having wanted to see his wife and son again,
---
إِبْرَاهِيم came to مَكَّة after إِسْمَاعِيل's marriage,
---
but he didn't find him at home. He asked إِسْمَاعِيل's wife about her husband and how they were doing. She complained of poverty,
---
so he asked her to tell إِسْمَاعِيل to change his doorstep. إِسْمَاعِيل understood the message,
---
divorced his wife and got married to the daughter of مُضَاض بْن عَمْرُو,
---
chief of the tribe of جُرْهُم.
---
Once more,
---
إِبْرَاهِيم came to see his son,
---
but again didn't find him at home. He asked his new wife the same previous question,
---
to which she thanked Allah. إِبْرَاهِيم asked her to tell إِسْمَاعِيل to keep his doorstep (i.e. to keep her as wife) and went back to فِلَسْطِين.
---
A third time,
---
إِبْرَاهِيم came to مَكَّة to find إِسْمَاعِيل sharpening an arrow under a lofty tree near زَمْزَم. The meeting,
---
after a very long journey of separation,
---
was very touching for a father so affectionate and a son so dutiful and righteous. This time,
---
father and son built الكَعْبَة and raised its pillars,
---
and إِبْرَاهِيم,
---
in compliance with Allah's Commandment,
---
called unto people to make pilgrimage to it.
---
By the grace of Allah,
---
إِسْمَاعِيل had twelve sons from the daughter of مُضَاض,
---
whose names were نَابِت,
---
قَيْذَار,
---
إِدْبِيل,
---
مِبْشَم,
---
مِشْمَع,
---
دُومَة,
---
مِيخَا,
---
هَدَد,
---
يَثْمَا,
---
يَثُور,
---
نَفِيش and قَيْدَمَان,
---
and who ultimately formed twelve tribes inhabiting مَكَّة and trading between يَمَن,
---
geographical شَام and مِصْر. Later on,
---
these tribes spread all over,
---
and even outside,
---
the peninsula. All their tidings went into oblivion except for the descendants of نَابِت and قَيْذَار.
---
The نَبَطِيُّون - sons of نَابِت - established a flourishing civilization in the north of حِجَاز,
---
they instituted a powerful government which spread out its domain over all neighbouring tribes,
---
and made بَتْرَاء their capital. Nobody dared challenge their authority until the Romans came and managed to eliminate their kingdom. After extensive research and painstaking investigation,
---
Mr. سُلَيْمَان النَّدْوِي came to the conclusion that the غَسَّانِي kings,
---
along with the أَوْس and خَزْرَج were not likely to be قَحْطَانِيِّين but rather نَبَطِيُّون.
---
Descendants of قَيْذَار,
---
the son of إِسْمَاعِيل,
---
lived long in مَكَّة increasing in number,
---
of them issued عَدْنَان and son مَعَدّ,
---
to whom عَدْنَانِيُّون traced back their ancestry. عَدْنَان is the twenty-first grandfather in the series of the Prophetic ancestry. It was said that whenever Prophet مُحَمَّد (Peace and blessings be upon him) spoke of his ancestry he would stop at عَدْنَان and say: 'Genealogists tell lies' and did not go farther than him. A group of scholars,
---
however,
---
favoured the probability of going beyond عَدْنَان attaching no significance to the aforementioned Prophetic Hadith. They went on to say that there were exactly forty fathers between عَدْنَان and إِبْرَاهِيم (Peace be upon them).
---
نِزَار,
---
مَعَدّ's only son,
---
had four sons who branched out into four great tribes; إِيَاد,
---
أَنْمَار,
---
رَبِيعَة and مُضَر. These last two sub-branched into several septs. رَبِيعَة fathered أَسَد,
---
عَنْزَة,
---
عَبْد القَيْس,
---
and وَائِل's two sons (بَكْر and تَغْلِب),
---
حَنِيفَة and many others.
---
مُضَر tribes branched out into two great divisions: قَيْس عَيْلَان بْن مُضَر and septs of إِلْيَاس بْن مُضَر. Of قَيْس عَيْلَان were the بَنُو سُلَيْم,
---
بَنُو هَوَازِن,
---
and بَنُو غَطَفَان of whom descended عَبْس,
---
ذُبْيَان,
---
أَشْجَع and غَنِيّ بْن أَعْصُر. Of إِلْيَاس بْن مُضَر were تَمِيم بْن مُرَّة,
---
هُذَيْل بْن مُدْرِكَة,
---
بَنُو أَسَد بْن خُزَيْمَة and septs of كِنَانَة بْن خُزَيْمَة,
---
of whom came قُرَيْش,
---
the descendants of فِهْر بْن مَالِك بْن النَّضْر بْن كِنَانَة.
---
قُرَيْش branched out into various tribes,
---
the most famous of whom were جُمَح,
---
سَهْم,
---
عَدِيّ,
---
مَخْزُوم,
---
تَيْم,
---
زُهْرَة and the three septs of قُصَيّ بْن كِلَاب: عَبْد الدَّار بْن قُصَيّ,
---
أَسَد بْن عَبْد العُزَّى بْن قُصَيّ and عَبْد مَنَاف بْن قُصَيّ.
---
عَبْد مَنَاف branched out into four tribes: عَبْد شَمْس,
---
نَوْفَل,
---
المُطَّلِب and هَاشِم. It is,
---
however,
---
from the family of هَاشِم that Allah selected Prophet مُحَمَّد بْن عَبْد Allah بْن عَبْد المُطَّلِب بْن هَاشِم (Peace and blessings be upon him).
---
Prophet مُحَمَّد (Peace and blessings be upon him) said: 'Allah selected إِسْمَاعِيل from the sons of إِبْرَاهِيم,
---
كِنَانَة from the sons of إِسْمَاعِيل,
---
قُرَيْش from the sons of كِنَانَة,
---
هَاشِم from the sons of قُرَيْش and He selected me from the sons of هَاشِم.'
---
العَبَّاس بْن عَبْد المُطَّلِب quoted the Messenger of Allah (Peace and blessings be upon him) as saying: 'Allah created mankind and chose me from the best whereof,
---
He chose the tribes and selected me from the best whereof; and He chose families and selected me from the best whereof. I am the very best in person and family.'
---
Having increased in number,
---
children of عَدْنَان,
---
in pursuit of pastures and water,
---
spread out over various parts of Arabia.
---
The tribe of عَبْد القَيْس,
---
together with some septs of بَكْر بْن وَائِل and تَمِيم,
---
emigrated to بَحْرَيْن where they dwelt. بَنُو حَنِيفَة بْن صَعْب بْن عَلِيّ بْن بَكْر went to settle in حَجَر,
---
the capital of يَمَامَة. All the tribes of بَكْر بْن وَائِل lived in an area of land which included يَمَامَة,
---
بَحْرَيْن,
---
سَيْف كَاظِمَة,
---
the sea shore,
---
the outer borders of عِرَاق,
---
أُبُلَّة and هِيت.
---
Most of the tribe of تَغْلِب lived in the Euphrates area while some of them lived with بَكْر. بَنُو تَمِيم lived in بَصْرَة semi-desert. بَنُو سُلَيْم lived in the vicinity of مَدِينَة on the land stretching from وَادِي القُرَى to خَيْبَر onwards to the eastern mountains to حَرَّة.
---
ثَقِيف dwelt in طَائِف and هَوَازِن east of مَكَّة near أَوْطَاس on the road from مَكَّة to بَصْرَة. بَنُو أَسَد lived on the land east of تَيْمَاء and west of كُوفَة,
---
while family of طَيِّئ lived between بَنُو أَسَد and تَيْمَاء. They were five-day-walk far from كُوفَة. ذُبْيَان inhabited the plot of and between تَيْمَاء and حَوْرَان.
---
Some septs of كِنَانَة lived in تِهَامَة,
---
while septs of قُرَيْش dwelt in مَكَّة and its suburbs. قُرَيْش remained completely disunited until قُصَيّ بْن كِلَاب managed to rally their ranks on honourable terms attaching major prominence to their status and importance.
